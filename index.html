The Case For Solar

Utility rates rise.
Sunlight doesn't.

Four reasons a solar array is one of the smartest upgrades you'll make this decade — for a home or a business.

01

Escape rising rates.

Utility bills climbed ~4.3% annually over the past decade. A paid-off solar array locks your generation cost at zero for decades.

02

Own — don't rent.

Every dollar to the utility disappears. Every kWh your panels produce is equity in your property. Solar homes sell for ~4% more on average.

03

Commercial & rural incentives.

Commercial Benefits

The incentive stack is
still strong for business.

While residential tax credits expired at the end of 2025, commercial projects retain multiple powerful financial incentives.

Federal · Active

30% Investment Tax Credit

The 48E ITC returns 30% of your project cost as a federal tax credit. Available for projects that begin construction by July 4, 2026, or are placed in service by December 31, 2027.

Federal · Active

Accelerated Depreciation (MACRS)

Solar equipment qualifies for 5-year Modified Accelerated Cost Recovery, dramatically improving after-tax ROI in the first few years.

USDA · Rural

REAP Grant

Rural Energy for America Program grants cover up to 50% of project cost for qualifying agricultural producers and rural small businesses. We handle the application.

State · Varies

State & Utility Programs

Property tax exemptions, sales tax exemptions, performance-based incentives, and net metering rules vary by state. We identify what applies to your site.

Incentives & Financing

The full picture
on solar incentives in 2026.

The federal landscape changed at the end of 2025. Here's exactly what's still available — for homes, for businesses, and for rural properties.

Important: The residential federal tax credit expired Dec 31, 2025

The 25D Residential Clean Energy Credit ended for homeowner purchases after December 31, 2025. Commercial projects (48E ITC) remain eligible through 2027, and residential customers can still access federal incentives through third-party ownership arrangements like leases and PPAs. Logic Solar walks every customer through the exact incentive stack that applies to their situation.

Federal Incentives

What's still on the table.

Commercial · Active

30% Investment Tax Credit (48E)

Commercial and utility-scale solar qualifies for a 30% federal income tax credit. Projects must begin physical construction by July 4, 2026 or be placed in service by December 31, 2027.

Learn more at IRS.gov →
Rural Business · Active

USDA REAP Grant

The Rural Energy for America Program provides grants up to 50% of total project cost for qualifying agricultural producers and rural small businesses. Logic Solar prepares and submits REAP applications on your behalf.

Learn more at USDA.gov →
Residential · Ended

25D Residential Credit

The 30% residential tax credit expired December 31, 2025 for homeowner-purchased systems. Systems installed before that date can still be claimed on 2025 tax returns.

TPO · Active

Third-Party Ownership

Solar leases and PPAs (where a third party owns the system) still qualify for federal incentives through end of 2027. The savings flow to homeowners as lower monthly payments.

State & Local

It varies by state.

Your state-level incentives can substantially reduce your out-of-pocket cost. We identify everything you qualify for and handle the paperwork.

Common

Net Metering

Excess energy your panels produce is sent back to the grid and credited against your future bills — essentially using the grid as a free battery. Available in all five states we serve.

Varies

Property Tax Exemptions

Many states exempt the value solar adds to your property from reassessment — you get the resale boost without a higher property tax bill.

Varies

Sales Tax Exemption

Several states we serve exempt solar equipment purchases from state sales tax, saving thousands on larger systems.

Varies

Utility Rebates

Some utilities offer direct rebates for solar and battery installations. These change frequently — we check every current program during your consultation.

Financing

No money down options.

If you'd rather not pay cash upfront, we offer financing from trusted lenders with terms starting at 5–25 years and APRs as low as 4.99% for qualified buyers.
